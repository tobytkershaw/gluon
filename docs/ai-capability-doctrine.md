# AI Capability Doctrine

## Purpose

Gluon should impose a small set of hard product constraints, then maximize the AI's freedom and usefulness inside those boundaries.

The goal is not to keep adding restrictions to the AI. The goal is to make the AI more capable, more legible, and more musically effective without weakening the collaboration contract.

## Hard Boundaries

These constraints are product-defining and should remain explicit:

- the human's hands win
- the AI acts when asked
- permission boundaries are enforced
- AI actions are inspectable
- AI actions are undoable
- execution remains bounded and coherent

These are the fixed edges of the instrument, not tuning knobs to revisit casually.

## Default Design Posture

Once the hard boundaries are in place, Gluon should bias toward empowering the AI rather than narrowing it.

That means:

- prefer real tools and operations over manual workarounds
- prefer richer state and better feedback over tighter prompt policing
- prefer composable capabilities over narrow one-off workflows
- treat weak AI behavior as a design problem in the environment before treating it as proof the AI should be allowed to do less

## Product Test

When evaluating a new feature or limitation, ask:

1. Is this protecting a core boundary, or just compensating for a weak interface?
2. Could the AI succeed here if the state, tools, or feedback were better?
3. Are we removing unnecessary friction from the AI's action space?
4. Are we increasing capability without reducing human authority?

If a restriction does not protect one of the hard boundaries, the default move should be to replace it with a better affordance.

## Summary

Constrain the AI at the product boundary.

Empower it aggressively inside that boundary.
